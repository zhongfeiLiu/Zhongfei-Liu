Regulating Spin State of Fe(Ⅲ) by Atomically Anchoring on Ultrathin Titanium Dioxide for Efficient Oxygen Evolution Electrocatalysis

2020-07-16

highlights:

  • The catalyst exhibits superior performance for OER with overpotential270 mV @10 mA/cm2 and 376 mV @100 mA/cm 2  in alkaline, which is much better than IrO2/C and RuO2/C and is the best iron-based OER catalyst free of active metals like Ni, Co or precious metals. This work is expected to provide a new way to develop cost-effective and high efficient iron-based electrocatalysts.
  • The absorption strength of oxygen-related intermediates on electrocatalyst is the key for smooth OER, and previous works reveal that the eg orbital of transition metal (i.e. Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni) participate the σ-bonding with oxygen-related anion, and its occupancy can greatly influence the bonding strength and thus OER activity. Fe(Ⅲ) has too high eg filling (~2.0) possesses poor absorption ability and shows bad performance.1 Ultradispersed Fe(Ⅲ) species with low spin state are expected to possess enhanced OER activity. However facile regulation on spin state is still very challenging.
