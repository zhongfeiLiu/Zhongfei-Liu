IrOOH nanosheets as acid stable electrochemical catalyst for the oxygen evolution reaction

2020-07-19

DOI:10.1039/c8ta07950a   Daniel Weber; Bettina V. Lotsch

Highlight

  • They present the synthesis of single layer IrOOH nanosheets and investigation of the their structure as well as their electrochemical properties towards oxygen evolution in aqueous acid conditions.

  • When incorporated as an active component in Ti electrode, the nanosheets exhibit a Tafel slope of 58(3)mV dec-1 and an overpotential of η10 mA cm-2=344(7)mV in 0.1M HClO4, while retaining the trivalent state of iridium. They outperform bulk rutile-IrO2 and bulk IrOOH as electrocatalytic water oxidation catalysts under the same conditions.

Experimental

  • Bulk IrOOH exfoliated via the tetrabutylammonium hydroxide(TBAOH). and the rare oxidation state of Ir observed in the bulk material is remained.

  • IrOOH nanosheets were prepared by an ultrsonication-assisted exfoliation of bulk IrOOH (obtained by acid treatment of K0.75Na0.25IrO2) in an aqueous TBAOH solution and subsequent washing. The nanosheets were stabilized by a zeta-potential of -36.4mV, indicating a negatively charged suface as well as an intermediates dispersion stability versus agglomeration. Dispersion of the monolayer were stable against agglomeration for 4~6 weeks.

  • K0.75Na0.25IrO2. 38.2mg(0.2mmol) iridium, a flux of 82mg(0.6mmol) 90 wt% K2CO3 and 10 wt% Na2CO3, ground them into a mixture and then heated in a corundum crucible in air for 15h at 900 °C. the product was quenched with water to room temperature and quickly transfered to an argon filled glove box. the resulting dark grey, powdery mixture.

  • The mixture of K0.75Na0.25IrO2 and remaining flux was washed with 1M HCl to exchange the alkali metal ions with protons and to removed the remaining carbonate flux as well as the soluble side phases. The mixture was shaken using an orbital shaker for 5 d; the supernatant acid was decanted and exchanged daily. The final product was washed with water and dried at 100℃ for 30minutes, violet metallic powder.

  • IrOOH(33.8 mg; 1.5 mmol） was soaked in a aqueous solution of TBAOH with a molar ration of TBAOH : IrOOH of 2:1. After ultrasonicating the dispersion for 30min, it was centrifuged at 1000 rpm and the supernatant was seperated from the unexfoliated solid components. Subsequently, the supernatant dispersion was centrifuged at 15000 rpm for 30min to precipitate the nanosheets, and the TBAOH solution was decanted and exchanged for doubly distilled H2O. yield a dark blue dispersion. This exchange of the TBAOH-containing supernatant for water was critical, as the nanosheets deposited from a TBAOH containing solution redispersed after deposition. impeding their electrochemical characterization.
